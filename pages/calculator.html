What Is a Financial Health Score?

A Financial Health Score is a single number — between 0 and 100 — that summarises how well you are managing your personal finances. It's calculated from six key metrics that financial experts use to evaluate individual financial stability.

Think of it like a credit score, but instead of measuring your creditworthiness to a bank, it measures your overall financial resilience and risk level. A higher score means you're better protected against financial shocks like job loss, medical emergencies, or sudden expenses.

How Is the Score Calculated?

Our algorithm evaluates six dimensions of your personal finance:

  • Expense Ratio — What percentage of your income goes to regular expenses?
  • EMI Burden — What share of your income is committed to loan repayments?
  • Emergency Fund Coverage — How many months of expenses can your savings cover?
  • Debt-to-Income Ratio — How does your total debt compare to your annual income?
  • Savings Surplus — Are you consistently saving a healthy percentage of income?
  • Dependent Load — How many people rely on your income?

Score Interpretation

  • 70–100 (Low Risk) — Strong financial position. Continue building wealth.
  • 40–69 (Moderate Risk) — Some vulnerabilities exist. Action recommended.
  • 0–39 (High Risk) — Significant financial stress. Immediate changes needed.

India-Specific Benchmarks

Unlike generic Western financial tools, our scoring uses benchmarks calibrated for Indian realities — including average income levels, inflation patterns, typical loan structures (home loans with longer tenures), and the unique role of family financial dependencies in Indian households.
